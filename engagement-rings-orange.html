The Right Precious Metal

The metal you choose impacts your engagement ring's appearance and durability.
  • Platinum: Known for its strength and resistance to tarnish, platinum is ideal for active lifestyles. Its naturally white color complements colorless diamonds and requires minimal maintenance. Platinum settings are a practical choice for those who work with their hands or prefer a ring that maintains its appearance over time.
  • Yellow Gold: This traditional metal has been used in jewelry for centuries. Its rich color pairs well with vintage designs and colored gemstones like emeralds and rubies. Yellow gold can develop a patina over time, giving the ring a distinct character. It works well for those who appreciate classic styles..
  • White Gold: White gold offers the look of platinum at a more accessible price and is a popular choice for modern designs, pairing well with diamonds and sapphires.
  • Rose Gold: Made by blending gold with copper, rose gold has a pinkish hue that offers a subtle, distinctive look. It pairs beautifully with stones like morganite or diamonds for a soft, romantic appearance. Rose gold is also more resistant to tarnish due to its copper content, making it a good option for daily wear.
